Old Technology

2018-07-01

For as long as I can remember, I've had a fascination with old technology.

One of my favorite old technologies is the typewriter. They have a lot of intricate, neat engineering, and are fun to type on. My friend Nick and I had a typewriter letter club going on for a while until he moved to Macedonia, and it was super fun. My typewriter is an Olympia SM3, I think from about 1956. It's really heavy, and the minimalist in me is loathe to get another of these monstrosities, but I also wish I had one of the IBM Selectrics, with their wonderful typeballs.

Another of my favorite old technologies is the text editor. I've been using Emacs (originally from 1976!) for the past five years or so. It's from a different era, and it shows, but with some elbow grease, it can be transformed into an editor that rivals, and in some ways even surpasses modern editors. By "some" elbow grease, I of course mean "a ludicrous amount of" elbow grease; attempting to use this editor for modern development isn't worth your time unless you're interested in the editor itself for some bizarre reason (like me :). Lately I've been playing around with Vim a bit more (which incidentally is also from 1976). I already like its text navigation more than Emacs, but I miss having elisp everywhere.
