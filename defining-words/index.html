Word definition popups with Linux and Sway

2020-03-28

One thing I really like on OSX is the thing where you three-finger tap a word to look up its definition. It's really nice to be able to get the definition so easily, without having to context switch, open up a new tab, or anything.

It got me thinking about whether or not it would be possible to do something similar in Linux. I didn't really want to start messing with touchpad stuff yet, so I thought I would try the slightly heavier user workflow of selecting, ctrl+c to copy into the clipboard, then meta+c to get the definition as a OS popup notification.

I found this, which is essentially what I just described, and looks pretty nice. I thought I would create an alternative because that one is A) hardcoded to use xclip, and I wanted to use wl-clip, and B) I thought I could replicate the entire thing with a couple lines of bash.

So, here you go!

#!/usr/bin/env bash

WORD=$(wl-paste)

curl -d "entry=$WORD" 'https://www.twinword.com/api/v4/word/definition/' | jq '.meaning | map_values(select(length > 0)) | join("\n")' | xargs notify-send --expire-time=10000 "$WORD"

As long as you don't go over 10k requests per month, the twinword API is free to use, and now you can get definitions pretty easily!
